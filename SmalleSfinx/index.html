  1. Seaming Issues

    1. Chuck flange vs can countersink
      1. Whilst researching broader double seaming resources online, I noticed frequent reference to a metric we've not been measuring: countersink
      2. We don't have the specific measuring tool for this metric, so I did my best to improvise one
      3. [image link]
      4. Seems like our can countersinks are a fair bit shallower than the chuck flange, but all the resources I could find suggested countersink is only a problem if it's too deep.
      5. Cask doesn't include measuring countersink in it's tutorials and guidelines, is it unnecessary?
    2. Seam measurement
      1. Since starting, I've been taking 4 measurments of each dimension in seam assessments, but then averaging it. Whilst reviewing a Canadian government food regulatory board document, I saw that they stressed not to do so. Moreover, an industry seaming record sheet I found online corroborated this by having min/max columns for various measurements. As such, all historical data on our seams taken to date is of questionable use, given that it's all average (pulling outliers back towards the mean)
    3. Seaming die
      1. Our seaming die and chuck bearings have never been stripped, cleaned, or replaced
      2. There's no discernable lateral or vertical wiggling when manipulated, nor visible scoring
      3. The die visibly need a deep clean
      4. The gap between closed die and chuck are difficult to measure, the cask manual advises putting the machine into 'hand'mode, but to my knowledge no such mode exists on the operating system we're running (different UI to the one in the manual). As such, I can't keep the die in the closed position for more than a second. Additionally, it's a tight squeeze and looks like it might require a dedicated tool.
      5. D1 not spinning as freely as D2 [video link]
    4. Can observations

  2. Filling Issues

    1. Foamfob troubles with high alcohol and 'sticky' beers
      1. Certain strong, usually dark, often sweet beers can't be made to foam at regular pressures and temperatures
      2. Well, they foam, but more like a guinness: gradually, and over a period of being something between beer and foam.
      3. Obviously this doesn't serve the purpose of a protective foam fob. We've found mixed success in filling at very high pressure (2bar), and filling warmer.
      4. No so much a technical issue, just curious if you had experience or insight on this issue, and any tips
    2. Pusharm 1 sometimes sticks, popping violently into position when manipulated
      1. Not sure how or why it's jamming/sticking, but light manipulation tends to free it (whereupon it jerks suddenly into position).
      2. I've found extending the arm and rinsing the cyllinder behind moderately effective at temporarily resolving this issue.
    3. Aluminium O-rings & rear fastening caps corroded and calcified (just need replacing I guess)
    4. Can food-safe grease and lube be used on the lid chute?
    5. Coils are going great, recurring pinch problem solved with cable management [image link]
